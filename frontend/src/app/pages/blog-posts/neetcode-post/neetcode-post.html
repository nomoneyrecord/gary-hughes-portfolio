What NeetCode Is Teaching Me About Patterns

Learning data structures and algorithms has become much more manageable once I stopped treating each problem like a separate trick and started focusing on reusable patterns.

One of the biggest shifts in my thinking has been realizing that many coding problems are less about memorizing exact solutions and more about recognizing the structure underneath them. Arrays, two pointers, sliding window, hash maps, stacks, and recursion all represent repeatable ways of thinking.

NeetCode has been useful because it organizes problems by pattern instead of just difficulty. That changes the learning experience. Instead of seeing a wall of unrelated exercises, I can start to identify which tool fits which kind of problem and why.

The value for me is not just solving interview questions. It is strengthening my ability to read a problem, identify constraints, choose a structure, and reason through tradeoffs more deliberately. That has value far beyond interviews.

My goal is to keep building fluency through repetition until these patterns feel natural enough to recognize quickly and explain clearly.
